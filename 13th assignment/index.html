1. Butter Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 500g boneless chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a pan, melt the butter and sauté the onion, garlic, and ginger until fragrant.
  2. Add the chicken pieces and cook until browned.
  3. Add garam masala, chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  4. Pour in the tomato puree and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Add the heavy cream and cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  6. Serve hot with naan or rice.

2. Masala Dosa

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (split black lentils)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for cooking
  • For masala filling: boiled and mashed potatoes, onion, green chili, ginger, turmeric, mustard seeds, curry leaves

Instructions:

  1. Soak the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds in water for at least 6 hours.
  2. Grind the soaked ingredients into a smooth batter, adding water as needed.
  3. Add salt to the batter and let it ferment overnight.
  4. Heat a non-stick pan and pour a ladleful of batter onto it, spreading it in a circular motion to make a thin dosa.
  5. Drizzle oil around the edges and cook until golden brown.
  6. Place a spoonful of the masala filling on one side of the dosa, fold it, and serve hot with chutney and sambar.

3. Palak Paneer

Ingredients:

  • 200g paneer (Indian cottage cheese), cubed
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 cups spinach, blanched and pureed
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and fry the paneer cubes until golden brown. Set aside.
  2. In the same pan, sauté the onion, garlic, and ginger until translucent.
  3. Add cumin powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well.
  4. Add the spinach puree and cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Add the fried paneer cubes and garam masala. Cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  6. Serve hot with roti or rice.

4. Biryani

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 250g chicken, cut into pieces (or vegetables for a vegetarian version)
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • A pinch of saffron strands soaked in milk
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Wash the rice and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat ghee in a pan and sauté the onion, garlic, and ginger until golden brown.
  3. Add the chicken (or vegetables) and cook until partially done.
  4. Add biryani masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Add yogurt and cook for 5 minutes.
  6. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil and add the soaked rice. Cook until the rice is 70% cooked. Drain the water.
  7. In a large pot, layer the partially cooked rice and the chicken (or vegetable) mixture. Repeat the layers.
  8. Drizzle the saffron milk on top and cover the pot tightly.
  9. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes until the rice is fully cooked and flavors are infused.
  10. Fluff the rice gently before serving.

5. Chole Bhature

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup chickpeas (chole), soaked overnight and cooked
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • For bhature (fried bread): 2 cups all-purpose flour, 1/2 cup yogurt, 1 teaspoon sugar, 1 teaspoon baking powder, oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté the onion, garlic, and ginger until golden brown.
  2. Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates.
  3. Add cumin powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  4. Add the cooked chickpeas and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. For the bhature, mix all-purpose flour, yogurt, sugar, and baking powder in a bowl. Knead into a soft dough and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  6. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them into flat circles.
  7. Heat oil in a deep pan and fry the bhature until they puff up and turn golden brown.
  8. Serve the chole with hot bhature and garnish with chopped onions and lemon wedges.
