List of Landmarks

  • Red Fort

    Location: Delhi, India

    Description: The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, is a historic fortification located in Old Delhi. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan and served as the main residence of the emperors of the Mughal dynasty. The Red Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its stunning red sandstone architecture.

  • Taj Mahal

    Location: Agra, India

    Description: The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum situated on the right bank of the Yamuna River in Agra. It was built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal and is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Taj Mahal is considered one of the most magnificent examples of Mughal architecture.

  • Statue of Unity

    Location: Gujarat, India

    Description: The Statue of Unity is a colossal statue of Indian political leader Vallabhbhai Patel, located near the Sardar Sarovar Dam in Gujarat. It is the world's tallest statue, standing at a height of 182 meters. The statue is a tribute to Patel's role in the country's independence movement and his efforts to unite India.

  • Gateway of India

    Location: Mumbai, India

    Description: The Gateway of India is an arch monument located on the waterfront in the city of Mumbai. It was built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to Bombay (now Mumbai) in 1911. The Gateway of India has since become a popular tourist attraction and a symbol of Mumbai's rich colonial history.

  • Taj Hotel

    Location: Mumbai, India

    Description: The Taj Mahal Palace Hotel, commonly known as the Taj Hotel, is a luxury hotel located in Mumbai, overlooking the Arabian Sea. It is considered one of the finest examples of Indo-Saracenic architecture and has been an iconic landmark in Mumbai since its establishment in 1903.
